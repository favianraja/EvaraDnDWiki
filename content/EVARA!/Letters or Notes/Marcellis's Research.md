> Entry 17 – A Soul’s Echo

They say death is silence. But I’ve heard the opposite—quiet breaths after hearts stop, shadows moving in the corner of still rooms. I don’t believe a soul disappears. I believe it waits. Not forever… but long enough to be caught.

The soul lingers. Long enough for questions. Long enough for pain.

> Entry 21 – Notes on Preservation

There is potential in elemental suspension—a mixture of ice and salt slows necrotic decay. It buys time. Not salvation. A suspended body still loses its soul.

My latest attempt failed. He spoke, but not like himself. Not with memory. Just instinct. Hunger. Souls are not clocks. We cannot rewind them.

Red Ink:
“You didn’t try hard enough.”
